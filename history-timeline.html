Civilization as a System

Human history is not just a sequence of dates. It is a feedback loop between biology, climate, energy, power, and information. When those layers reinforce each other, civilizations rise. When they fall out of alignment, collapse follows.

  • Integration: trade, language, agriculture, and law bind groups together.
  • Complexity: cities, empires, priesthoods, armies, and supply chains emerge.
  • Fragility: drought, war, migration, disease, or resource stress can cascade.
  • Reorganization: collapse rarely ends history — it rearranges it.
